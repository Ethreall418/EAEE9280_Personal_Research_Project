All four integration tests complete 288 steps (24 h) without
non-finite values. T and S remain within physically plausible ranges
in all cases. The validation confirms:

  1. ORAS5 loading and regridding pipeline works correctly for both
     a mid-latitude western boundary current region and a tropical
     open-ocean region.

  2. The model integrates stably with ORAS5-initialised fields at
     dt=300 s and DEPTH_MAX=500 m.

  3. The baroclinic cold-start (T/S only) is cleaner and more stable,
     particularly in the tropics, and is the recommended initialisation
     strategy for future validation runs.

Known limitation
----------------
  Full ORAS5 initialisation (including u, v, eta) at DEPTH_MAX=2000 m
  and dt=900 s is unstable. Root cause: the regridded velocity field
  (0.68 m/s Gulf Stream) creates barotropic transport divergence
  (~5 m/step of eta change) that exceeds the initial SSH (~1 m).
  This is a physical imbalance from grid coarsening, not a bug in
  the numerical scheme. The leapfrog free-surface fix (committed
  2026-03-25) correctly addresses the theoretical instability for
  small perturbations; large-perturbation runs require either a
  shallower domain, a smaller dt, or geostrophic initialisation.
